Erbium (Er)

Atomic Number 68 | A Rare Earth Metal with Optical and Magnetic Applications

Overview

Erbium is a chemical element with the symbol Er and atomic number 68. It is a soft, silvery metal that belongs to the lanthanide series of rare earth elements. Erbium is valued for its use in lasers and optical fibers.

Discovered in 1843 by Swedish chemist Carl Gustaf Mosander, erbium was isolated from the mineral gadolinite. Its unique pink coloration and optical properties make it an important material in modern technologies.

Properties & Uses

  • Atomic Number: 68
  • Atomic Mass: 167.26 u
  • Electron Configuration: [Xe] 4f12 6s2
  • Group / Block: Lanthanides (f-block)
  • Standard State: Solid at 25 °C
  • Melting Point: 1529 °C
  • Boiling Point: 2868 °C
  • Density: 9.07 g/cm³

Erbium is widely used as a dopant in fiber optic cables, where it amplifies signals for telecommunications. It is also used in laser technology, especially in medical and dental applications. Additionally, erbium is used in nuclear reactors as a neutron absorber.

Historical Perspective

Erbium was discovered in 1843 by Carl Gustaf Mosander during his analysis of the mineral gadolinite. Its name is derived from Ytterby, the Swedish village where the mineral was first found.

Today, erbium is a key material in optical and laser technologies, playing a significant role in telecommunications and medical advancements.
