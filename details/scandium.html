Scandium (Sc)

Atomic Number 21 | Transition Metal

Overview

Scandium is a chemical element with the symbol Sc and atomic number 21. It is a silvery-white metal and is classified as a transition metal. Scandium is relatively rare in the Earth's crust, often found in rare-earth deposits and uranium ores.

Discovered in 1879 by Lars Fredrik Nilson, scandium has limited applications but is used in aerospace components, sports equipment, and lighting.

Properties & Uses

  • Atomic Number: 21
  • Atomic Mass: 44.956 u
  • Electron Configuration: [Ar] 3d1 4s2
  • Group / Block: 3 (d-block)
  • Standard State: Solid at 25 °C
  • Melting Point: 1541 °C
  • Boiling Point: 2836 °C
  • Radioactive: No

Scandium is primarily used in alloys with aluminum for aerospace and sporting applications. It is also utilized in mercury vapor lamps and has potential applications in fuel cells and other high-tech industries.

Historical Perspective

Scandium was first identified in 1879 by Swedish chemist Lars Fredrik Nilson while analyzing rare-earth minerals. Its name is derived from "Scandia," the Latin name for Scandinavia.

Although scandium has relatively few applications compared to other metals, its unique properties make it valuable in niche markets like aerospace and sports equipment manufacturing.
