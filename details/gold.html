Gold (Au)

Atomic Number 79 | Precious and Malleable Metal

Overview

Gold is a chemical element with the symbol Au (from Latin: aurum) and atomic number 79. It is a highly sought-after metal due to its luster, malleability, and resistance to corrosion. Gold has been used as a medium of exchange, in jewelry, and in technology for millennia.

Known since ancient times, gold's unique properties have made it a symbol of wealth and power. It continues to hold significant value in modern finance, industry, and art.

Properties & Uses

  • Atomic Number: 79
  • Atomic Mass: 196.97 u
  • Electron Configuration: [Xe] 4f14 5d10 6s1
  • Group / Block: 11 (d-block)
  • Standard State: Solid at 25 °C
  • Melting Point: 1064.18 °C
  • Boiling Point: 2970 °C
  • Radioactive: No

Gold is extensively used in jewelry, coins, and bullion due to its aesthetic appeal and intrinsic value. It is also employed in electronics for its excellent conductivity and resistance to oxidation. Additionally, gold has medical applications in treatments and diagnostics, as well as in space technology for thermal shielding.

Gold in Action

Gold is a versatile element with a rich history and a bright future. Its unique properties and cultural significance have made it a valuable asset in various industries and applications.

Historical Perspective

Gold has been revered by civilizations since ancient times for its rarity and beauty. It has been used in everything from currency to religious artifacts. The gold standard once underpinned global economies, cementing its role as a valuable resource.

Today, gold continues to play a critical role in technology, investment, and luxury goods, symbolizing prosperity and innovation.
