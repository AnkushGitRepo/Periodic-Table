Dysprosium (Dy)

Atomic Number 66 | A Rare Earth Element Known for Its Magnetic Properties

Overview

Dysprosium is a chemical element with the symbol Dy and atomic number 66. It is a silvery-white, soft metal belonging to the lanthanide series. Dysprosium is known for its high magnetic strength and thermal neutron absorption.

Discovered in 1886 by French chemist Paul-Émile Lecoq de Boisbaudran, dysprosium's name derives from the Greek word "dysprositos," meaning "hard to get," reflecting the difficulty in isolating it from its ores.

Properties & Uses

  • Atomic Number: 66
  • Atomic Mass: 162.50 u
  • Electron Configuration: [Xe] 4f10 6s2
  • Group / Block: Lanthanides (f-block)
  • Standard State: Solid at 25 °C
  • Melting Point: 1407 °C
  • Boiling Point: 2562 °C
  • Density: 8.54 g/cm³

Dysprosium is widely used in high-performance magnets, particularly in electric motors and wind turbines. Its ability to absorb neutrons makes it a valuable material in nuclear reactors. Dysprosium-doped glass is also used in lasers.

Dysprosium in Action

Historical Perspective

Dysprosium was first isolated in 1886 by Paul-Émile Lecoq de Boisbaudran from holmium oxide. Its challenging extraction process reflects its name, meaning "hard to get." Advances in separation techniques have since made dysprosium more accessible.

Today, dysprosium plays a crucial role in clean energy technologies and high-performance electronics, underlining its importance in modern science and industry.
