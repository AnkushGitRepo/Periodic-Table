Samarium (Sm)

Atomic Number 62 | A Rare Earth Metal with Diverse Applications

Overview

Samarium is a chemical element with the symbol Sm and atomic number 62. It is a silvery-white metal and one of the rare earth elements belonging to the lanthanide series. Samarium is known for its role in specialized magnetic materials and nuclear reactors.

Discovered in 1879 by French chemist Paul-Émile Lecoq de Boisbaudran, samarium was named after the mineral samarskite, from which it was first isolated.

Properties & Uses

  • Atomic Number: 62
  • Atomic Mass: 150.36 u
  • Electron Configuration: [Xe] 4f6 6s2
  • Group / Block: Lanthanides (f-block)
  • Standard State: Solid at 25 °C
  • Melting Point: 1072 °C
  • Boiling Point: 1900 °C
  • Density: 7.52 g/cm³

Samarium is widely used in permanent magnets (samarium-cobalt magnets), which have high thermal stability. It is also used as a neutron absorber in nuclear reactors and as a catalyst in chemical reactions.

Historical Perspective

Samarium was first isolated in 1879 from the mineral samarskite by Paul-Émile Lecoq de Boisbaudran. The element was named after the mineral, which itself was named after Russian mining engineer Colonel Vasili Samarsky-Bykhovets.

Today, samarium is a key component in high-performance magnets and nuclear technologies, demonstrating its versatility and importance in modern science and industry.
