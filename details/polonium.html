Polonium (Po)

Atomic Number 84 | Rare and Radioactive Element

Overview

Polonium is a chemical element with the symbol Po and atomic number 84. It is a rare and highly radioactive metalloid. Polonium occurs naturally in uranium ores but is found in minute quantities. It is most famous for its intense radioactivity and was discovered by Marie and Pierre Curie in 1898.

Polonium is named after Poland ("Polonia" in Latin), Marie Curie’s homeland. It has very few practical applications but has been used in devices like anti-static brushes and as a heat source in space equipment.

Properties & Uses

  • Atomic Number: 84
  • Atomic Mass: 209 u
  • Electron Configuration: [Xe] 4f14 5d10 6s2 6p4
  • Group / Block: 16 (p-block)
  • Standard State: Solid at 25 °C
  • Melting Point: 254 °C
  • Boiling Point: 962 °C
  • Radioactive: Yes

Polonium has been used in devices such as anti-static brushes for removing dust on photographic films. Its radioactive properties make it suitable as a heat source in satellites and spacecraft. However, handling polonium requires extreme caution due to its toxicity and intense radioactivity.

Historical Perspective

Polonium was discovered by Marie and Pierre Curie in 1898 while they were investigating radioactive substances. It was the first element discovered by the Curies and was named in honor of Marie Curie’s homeland, Poland. Polonium’s discovery marked a milestone in the study of radioactivity.

Despite its rarity and dangers, polonium has played a role in scientific research and industrial applications. Its legacy is closely tied to the pioneering work of the Curies in understanding radioactive elements.
