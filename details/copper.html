Copper (Cu)

Atomic Number 29 | Essential Conductive Metal

Overview

Copper is a chemical element with the symbol Cu and atomic number 29. Known for its excellent thermal and electrical conductivity, copper is a reddish metal widely used in electrical wiring, plumbing, and industrial machinery.

Used by humans for thousands of years, copper was one of the first metals to be extracted and used in tools and ornaments. Its versatility and recyclability make it an indispensable material in modern technology.

Properties & Uses

  • Atomic Number: 29
  • Atomic Mass: 63.546 u
  • Electron Configuration: [Ar] 3d10 4s1
  • Group / Block: 11 (d-block)
  • Standard State: Solid at 25 °C
  • Melting Point: 1084.62 °C
  • Boiling Point: 2562 °C
  • Radioactive: No

Copper is extensively used in electrical wiring, electronics, and power generation due to its high conductivity. It is also a key material in plumbing, roofing, and industrial machinery. Copper alloys, such as bronze and brass, are widely used for their durability and aesthetic appeal.

Copper in Action

Copper is a versatile metal with a wide range of applications, from electrical wiring to architectural elements. Its unique properties make it an essential material in various industries, including construction, transportation, and renewable energy.

Historical Perspective

Copper has been used by humans for over 10,000 years, with early artifacts found in Mesopotamia and Egypt. Its name is derived from the Latin word "cuprum," meaning "from Cyprus," where it was extensively mined in ancient times.

Today, copper remains a critical material in modern infrastructure and technology, playing a vital role in renewable energy systems and electronics.
