Krypton (Kr)

Atomic Number 36 | Noble Gas

Overview

Krypton is a chemical element with the symbol Kr and atomic number 36. It is a colorless, odorless, and tasteless noble gas that is inert under most conditions. Krypton is found in trace amounts in the Earth's atmosphere and is commonly used in lighting and photography.

Discovered in 1898 by Sir William Ramsay and Morris Travers, krypton is often used in fluorescent lamps and as a filling gas in certain types of light bulbs.

Properties & Uses

  • Atomic Number: 36
  • Atomic Mass: 83.798 u
  • Electron Configuration: [Ar] 3d10 4s2 4p6
  • Group / Block: 18 (p-block)
  • Standard State: Gas at 25 °C
  • Melting Point: −157.37 °C
  • Boiling Point: −153.22 °C
  • Radioactive: No

Krypton is primarily used in fluorescent lamps and in certain photographic flashes for high-speed photography. It is also used as a filling gas in specialized gas discharge tubes and in the production of insulating windows.

Historical Perspective

Krypton was discovered in 1898 by Sir William Ramsay and Morris Travers while studying the residues left from evaporating liquid air. Its name is derived from the Greek word "kryptos," meaning hidden. Krypton's applications in lighting and scientific research highlight its significance as a noble gas.

Today, krypton is used in various technologies, from high-performance lighting to plasma displays and lasers.
